Multilingual Research Paper
PDF of Paper

I co-wrote an original research paper on multilingualism and emotional expression.

Background

To understand the paper, you’ll need to follow two chains of reasoning.

  • 1. Multilingual Cognition

    Here’s the first chain of reasoning. Experimental evidence demonstrates that multilingual individuals are better than monolingual individuals at complex rule-based decision making. Why? Well, quoting directly from the paper, “...multilinguals have had to learn the rules of multiple languages as well as learn to combine rules between and within them to avoid social missteps.”

  • 2. Self-Construals

    Here’s the second chain of reasoning. A self-construal is how a person views themselves and understands their relationship to the outside world. In Western culture, people frequently have what is called an independent self-construal. This means that they prize emotional expression as a way of asserting their distinctive personalities. In contrast, Easterners frequently have an interdependent self-construal. They value emotional suppression in order to maintain social harmony, because they believe personality and the self only makes sense “in light of the broader social context.”

  • 3. Putting it Together

    If multilingual individuals are better at complex rule-based cognition in general, they might be more adept at following social norms. Thus, we predicted that they would be more likely to express emotions “in accordance with their self-construals.” More specifically, if a multilingual person has an interdependent self-construal, they are going to suppress their emotions more than a monolingual interdependent person, while a multilingual person with an independent self-construal is more likely to express their emotions than a monolingual independent person. Let’s visualize the predictions here. The higher the number, the higher the emotional expressivity of people who fall into that group.

Research

To explore our hypothesis, we surveyed 316 undergraduate students with an online questionnaire. These students filled out a Self-Construal Scale, to find out whether they had independent or interdependent construals, and two questionnaires to measure their emotional expressivity.

I would normally just have you read the paper to get all the details, but honestly, it’s a pretty dense and technical read. So, I’ll lay out what we found here. After running statistical analyses, we found the following: Interdependent multilinguals suppressed their emotions more than interdependent monolinguals, but independent multilinguals did NOT report more emotional expression than independent multilinguals. That was probably pretty confusing to read. Once again, let’s map out our results. Remember, the higher the number, the higher the emotional expressivity of that group.

Communication

My co-writer Hailey actually came up with the hypothesis and ran the analyses. My job was to rewrite the Introduction, write the Results, and write the Discussion Section. As the target audience was fellow psychology researchers, my writing was very technical. Because the hypothesis was so complex, it was a challenge to keep the paper as lean and clear as possible. Frankly, I don’t think I succeeded in that, but I’ve tried to simplify the paper here on this page. That’s it!
